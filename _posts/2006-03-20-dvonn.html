I've played DVONN (which is a game from the GIPF project) once in Prague's board games club called Paluba. Now I started to play DVONN on littlegolem (no link needed for regular readers, right? :-)) and I found myself in trouble after noticing that I play it despite not even remembering the rules exactly.

Well, now I am trying to fill the gap, and I found out one interesting thing - my brain probably consists of a neural network because I was able to mimic my opponents' strategy even without knowing the rules... Now you might think that I spend my time talking about banalities, but have you thought about it thoroughly? I mean, I have no idea how neural networks really work although I've been trying to understand it, I have no idea how my own fscking brain works.
On a more positive note, my and your (however dumb ;-)) brain is still a wonderful thing capable of solving very difficult tasks. Congratulations!

I guess I'd better go to sleep already... (by the way... why is this post called DVONN?)
